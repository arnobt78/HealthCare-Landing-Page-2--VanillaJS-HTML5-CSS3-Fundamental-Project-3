Communication 6 min read Family Friendly

Better Questions, Better Care

Strong communication helps patients understand treatment plans, follow guidance, and return sooner when symptoms change.

  • Write down top 3 concerns before every appointment.
  • Ask for plain-language explanations of diagnosis and next steps.
  • Confirm medication timing, dosage, and warning signs.
  • Bring a family member when discussing complex care plans.

If something is unclear, ask your clinician to repeat it in simpler terms. Clarity is part of quality care.
